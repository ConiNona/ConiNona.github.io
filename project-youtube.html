Overview

I've been creating and editing video content for YouTube, managing every step from concept to publication. My work includes producing cooking videos for a family channel as well as short films I've directed and edited myself.

In addition, we created an alternate version of the cooking channel in English, where I recorded voiceovers and directed my mother to perform in Spanish. This added an extra layer of coordination and localization, allowing the same content to reach a wider audience.

Across these projects, I've gained hands-on experience in filming, multi-camera setups, lighting, directing, editing, post-production, and publishing content that engages audiences. It also gave me space to experiment with storytelling, technical workflows, and creative decision-making in real-world projects.

What I Learned & Achieved
  • Developed concepts and planned each video independently.
  • Directed on-camera action, guiding performance and pacing.
  • Filmed content with multiple cameras, controlling lighting, framing, and audio.
  • Edited videos using Lightworks initially, later transitioning to DaVinci Resolve.

Outcome

These projects helped me develop a complete skill set in video production and storytelling. The family cooking channel grew to over 6,000 subscribers, while the English version allowed us to reach a broader audience. The short films gave me the chance to explore narrative, visual style, and experimental techniques in a more creative context.

Reflection

Through these projects, I learned to manage the full production process on my own, from directing and filming to editing and publishing. Working independently taught me problem-solving, technical workflow, and creative decision-making.

I also learned to work with others, directing actors and team members to achieve a cohesive final product. Over time, I've expanded my skills in post-production, color grading, sound design, and animation, which I continue to develop on current and future projects.

The part that doesn't show up on screen

Most people see the finished video. What they don't see is the coordination: deciding what to cook that week, setting up lights in a home kitchen, directing a family member who isn't a performer, dealing with audio problems mid-shoot, and then sitting down for hours to make it all feel effortless. Doing that consistently over 8+ years, across two languages and multiple formats, is what actually built my skills. The channel was the project, but the process was the real education.
